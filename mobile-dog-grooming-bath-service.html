A Closer Look at Each Step

Warm Bath with Shampoo & Conditioner

We use gentle, eco-friendly products formulated for your dog's specific coat type—whether your pup is a wiry terrier, a double-coated shepherd, or a soft Labradoodle. Dogs with allergies or sensitive skin can be treated with hypoallergenic formulas upon request.

One client in Erie, a lab mix named Scout, had flaky skin and hated baths—until we switched to an oatmeal-based shampoo and bathed her right in her driveway. Now? She sits still for the massage.

Blow-Dry & Brush-Out

Most dogs aren't fans of big dryers blasting air at them. That's why we use adjustable-speed, low-noise dryers to gently fluff your dog dry. Then we brush them out fully to remove any loose fur or tangles.

For dogs prone to shedding, this step doubles as a light dog deshedding session. (If your dog is a fur-cannon like a husky or golden retriever, consider adding our “Fur-nado Deshed” upgrade.)

Ear Cleaning

Gunk in the ears? Happens more than you'd think, especially with floppy-eared breeds like doodles and cockers. We gently clean your dog's ears using safe, non-irritating solutions. If there's excessive buildup or your dog seems uncomfortable, we'll flag it and recommend a vet visit—because grooming is also about noticing small things before they become big ones.

Nail Trimming (with Dremel)

We don't just clip and go. We Dremel the nails smooth to reduce sharp edges and keep your floors (and your lap) scratch-free. Dogs who hate this step get extra patience—and a few liver treats. Over time, regular nail trimming can help the quick recede, making it easier to keep nails short without discomfort.

Teeth & Breath Refresh

This is one of those services that most pet parents forget about… until their dog yawns in their face. It's not the same as a dental vet cleaning, but it's a helpful step between visits—and it gets your dog used to having their mouth handled.

Paw Pad Shave Out

We gently trim excess hair from the paw pads so your dog has better grip on slick floors, especially in older dogs or those with mobility issues. This step also helps keep mud, salt, and ice from building up—especially useful during Colorado winters.

Why use a Mobile service?

You've got enough to manage. Our mobile dog groomer shows up outside your home in a fully equipped grooming van, so there's no driving, no waiting, and no stress. We're complete the groom in our groomingvan at your curb, leaving your pup clean and calm, not frazzled and exhausted.

Whether you're in Arvada, Northglenn, Westminster, or Thornton, our mobile grooming van brings spa-quality service right to your street.

Do you use clippers or a Dremel?

We use a Dremel (rotary grinder) for most nail trims because it gives a smoother finish and helps avoid cracking or splintering. It's also the best way to slowly shorten the nail without hitting the quick. That said, if your dog is more comfortable with traditional clippers—or if we need to start with clippers for longer nails—we'll adjust to what's safest and least stressful for your pup.
Our goal is always a clean, comfortable trim with as little drama as possible.
