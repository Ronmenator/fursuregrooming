Save time, money & stress with scheduled grooming

The time and cost of each grooming session depend largely on your dog's coat condition and how well it's maintained between appointments. Sticking to a regular grooming schedule keeps your pet's coat healthy and reduces the work (and cost) required each visit.

Frequent visits also allow us to catch skin irritations, coat issues, or signs of discomfort early—making treatment faster, easier, and more affordable. Most importantly, regular grooming supports your dog's overall wellness and keeps them looking and feeling their best.

How often should I groom my dog?

Dogs should be groomed regularly based on coat type, age, and lifestyle. Here's a general grooming schedule for different needs:

- Every 4-6 weeks for dogs with long hair or double coats.
- Every 6-8 weeks for dogs with short hair or smooth coats.
- Every 2-3 weeks for puppies, to help them get used to grooming.
- Every 3-4 weeks for nail trims, to maintain healthy paw pads and prevent overgrowth.
