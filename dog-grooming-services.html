Our 5-Step Grooming Experience
  1. Consultation & Pet Assessment. We start with a hands-on evaluation of your dog's coat, skin, and overall condition. Our mobile groomer customizes the treatment and discusses any special needs, from skin care to hygiene concerns.
  2. Bath & Massage (& add-On Services if Requested). Your pet enjoys a soothing dog bath using high-quality shampoos and conditioners matched to their coat type. A calming massage promotes relaxation and circulation. This stage also includes optional add-on grooming services such as deshedding treatments, teeth brushing, and anal gland expression, all available at your request.
  3. Drying with Care. Drying is typically the longest phase of the grooming process. We use adjustable-speed dryers to ensure safe, low-stress drying. Perfect for nervous pups or those sensitive to noise.
  4. Brushing, Nails, Ears and Paws. This step includes a full coat brushing to remove tangles and loose fur, followed by nail trimming and Dremel filing for a smooth, safe finish. We gently clean your dog's ears, and by request, remove ear hair. To protect and nourish the feet, we can apply Paw Butter to dry or cracked paw pads—a soothing treatment that enhances comfort and mobility.
  5. Finishing Touches. We complete the groom with a professional dog haircut—whether it's a quick tidy-trim or a full-body style—when included in your selected grooming package. Your pup leaves looking fresh, feeling great, and optionally wearing a spritz of pet cologne and a cute bow or bandana.

How often should I groom my dog?

Dogs should be groomed regularly based on coat type, age, and lifestyle. Here's a general grooming schedule for different needs:

- Every 4–6 weeks for dogs with long hair or double coats.
- Every 6–8 weeks for dogs with short hair or smooth coats.
- Every 2–3 weeks for puppies, to help them get used to grooming.
- Every 3–4 weeks for nail trims, to maintain healthy paw pads and prevent overgrowth.

What are the benefits of mobile dog grooming?

Mobile dog grooming offers a stress-free, convenient alternative to traditional salons. It eliminates travel time, reduces anxiety for pets, and allows one-on-one attention in a familiar environment. Grooming at home also lowers exposure to other animals and potential health risks.

Save time, money & stress with scheduled grooming

The time and cost of each grooming session depend largely on your dog's coat condition and how well it's maintained between appointments. Sticking to a regular mobile grooming schedule keeps your pet's coat healthy and reduces the work (and cost) required each visit.

Frequent visits also allow us to catch skin irritations, coat issues, or signs of discomfort early—making treatment faster, easier, and more affordable. Most importantly, regular grooming supports your dog's overall wellness and keeps them looking and feeling their best.
